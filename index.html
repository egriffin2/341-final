Pacuare Nature Reserve is a 2500 acre area of tropical rainforest on the Caribbean coast of Costa Rica. The reserve was estabilshed by the Endangered Wildlife Trust in 1989 and in 2016 was gifted to Ecology Project International. It is also the location of important nesting sites for leatherback sea turtles. Leatherback sea turtles are endangered, and one of Pacuare's focuses is to aid in conservation efforts for these animals. Leatherbacks are huge, weighing somewhere between 550 and 1500 lbs and growing to be anywhere from 6 - 7.2 feet in length as adults.
Pacuare is open to volunteers who want to take part in the everyday activity of the reserve. Volunteers accompany research assistants on nightly patrols to look for nesting mothers and hatching nests, as well as taking part in recycling, maintenance, composting, and reforestation.
